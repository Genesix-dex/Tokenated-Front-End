Imprints

What are Imprints?

Imprints are Tokenated's social engagement feature that allows you to show appreciation for NFTs you admire. Similar to "likes" on social platforms, Imprints help creators gain visibility and allow collectors to bookmark their favorite pieces.

How to Imprint an NFT

To Imprint an NFT, simply click the Imprint icon displayed on any NFT card or detail page. Your Imprint is recorded on-chain, creating a permanent connection between you and the artwork you appreciate.

Benefits of Imprints

Imprinting NFTs helps boost their visibility in search results and trending collections. For collectors, your Imprinted items are saved in your profile's "Imprints" tab, making it easy to revisit pieces you've shown interest in. Some creators also offer special benefits to users who Imprint their collections.

Imprint Analytics

Creators can access Imprint analytics in their dashboard to see which pieces are receiving the most engagement. This data can help inform future creative decisions and pricing strategies.

Request Functionality

What is the Request feature?

The Request feature allows you to express interest in purchasing NFTs that aren't currently listed for sale. This signals to owners that there's demand for their NFTs and encourages them to consider listing.

How to request an unlisted NFT

When viewing an unlisted NFT's detail page, click the "Request to List" button. You'll be prompted to enter an offer price (optional) and a message to the owner explaining your interest. The owner will receive a notification of your request.

As an NFT owner

If you own an NFT and receive listing requests, you'll be notified in your dashboard and via email (if enabled). You can view all requests, including offered prices, and choose to list your NFT directly from the request notification.

Request limits

To prevent spam, there's a limit of 10 active requests per user per day. Additionally, owners can choose to disable requests for specific NFTs in their collection settings.

Privacy considerations

When you make a request, your profile information is shared with the NFT owner. Your offered price is only visible to the owner, not to other users who may also be requesting the same NFT.

Wallet Issues

How do I connect my wallet?

Click the "Connect Wallet" button in the top right corner of the page. You'll see options for MetaMask, WalletConnect and MetaMask Select your preferred wallet and follow the prompts to connect.

Why is my transaction pending?

Transactions may be pending due to network congestion or if you've set a gas fee that's too low. You can speed up the transaction by increasing the gas fee through your wallet's settings.

I don't see my NFTs in my wallet

Make sure you're connected to the correct network (Base) and that your wallet supports viewing NFTs. Some wallets require you to manually add the NFT contract address to view your tokens.

How do I manage multiple wallets?

You can connect multiple wallets to your account in your profile settings. This allows you to view and manage assets across different wallets from a single dashboard.

Buying NFTs

How to purchase an NFT

Browse collections, find an NFT you like, and click "Buy Now" or "Place Bid" depending on the listing type. Confirm the purchase in your wallet when prompted and wait for the transaction to complete.

Understanding auctions

For auction listings, you'll need to place a bid above the current highest bid. If you win, you'll be prompted to complete the purchase when the auction ends. If you're outbid, your funds will be returned to your wallet.

What are gas fees?

Gas fees are transaction costs paid to the network for processing your purchase. These fees vary based on network congestion. We recommend buying during off-peak hours to save on gas fees.

Failed transactions

If your transaction fails, you may still be charged gas fees even though the purchase wasn't completed. This is because the network still processed your transaction attempt. Try again with a higher gas limit or during lower congestion times.

Creating NFTs

How to mint an NFT

Go to the "Create" section, upload your digital asset (image, video, audio, etc.), fill in the details like title, description, and properties, select your blockchain, and click "Create". Confirm the transaction in your wallet.

File types and sizes

We support JPG, PNG, GIF, SVG, MP4, WEBM, MP3, WAV, OGG, GLB, GLTF for NFTs. Maximum file size is 100MB for most types. For optimal performance, we recommend keeping files under 50MB.

Selling NFTs

How to list an NFT for sale

Go to your profile, select the NFT you want to sell, click "Sell", choose between fixed price or timed auction, set your price and duration, and confirm the listing. You'll need to approve the marketplace to sell your NFT.

Setting the right price

Research similar NFTs in your category to determine a competitive price. Consider your following, the uniqueness of your work, and current market conditions. Starting with a lower price can help attract initial buyers.

Royalties

Royalties allow you to earn a percentage of all future sales of your NFT. You can set royalties (typically 5-10%) when creating a collection or individual NFT. These royalties are automatically paid to you when your NFT is resold.

Receiving payments

When your NFT sells, the payment (minus platform fees) will be sent directly to your connected wallet. This can be instant or take a few minutes to process.

Security

Protecting your wallet

Never share your private keys or seed phrase with anyone. Use a hardware wallet for large holdings. Enable two-factor authentication where available, and be careful about which sites you connect your wallet to.

Avoiding scams

Be wary of offers that seem too good to be true, messages asking you to urgently send cryptocurrency, fake websites, and unsolicited NFT airdrops. Always verify the authenticity of collections before purchasing.

Reporting suspicious activity

If you encounter a scam or suspicious activity, report it immediately through our "Report" feature. Provide as many details as possible to help our security team investigate the issue.

Payments

Payment options

We accept Ethereum (ETH), Polygon (MATIC), and select ERC-20 tokens for purchases. You can also use credit/debit cards through our fiat on-ramp partners for select NFTs.

Cryptocurrency conversions

If you need to convert between cryptocurrencies, you can use integrated swap features in many wallets or external exchanges. Be aware of conversion fees and price slippage during swaps.

Understanding transaction fees

Our platform charges a 2.5% fee on all transactions. Additionally, you'll pay gas fees to the blockchain network. Gas fees vary based on network congestion and complexity of the transaction.

Tax implications

Buying and selling NFTs may have tax implications in your jurisdiction. We recommend consulting with a tax professional about reporting requirements for cryptocurrency transactions and NFT sales.

Wallet Issues

How do I connect my wallet to Tokenated?

To connect your wallet to Tokenated:

  1. Click the "Connect Wallet" button in the top-right corner of the page.
  2. Select your preferred wallet provider (MetaMask, WalletConnect.).
  3. Follow the prompts in your wallet to authorize the connection.
  4. Once connected, your wallet address will appear in the navigation bar.

If you encounter any issues, make sure your wallet is properly set up and you have the necessary browser extensions installed.

Why won't my transactions confirm?

Transaction issues could be due to several factors:

  • Insufficient gas fees: Try increasing your gas limit for faster confirmation.
  • Network congestion: During peak times, transactions may take longer to process.
  • Low balance: Ensure you have enough cryptocurrency to cover both the purchase and gas fees.
  • Wallet connection issues: Try refreshing the page or reconnecting your wallet.

If problems persist, check your transaction status on the blockchain explorer or contact our support team.

Which wallets are supported on Tokenated?

Tokenated currently supports the following wallets:

  • MetaMask
  • WalletConnect

We're continuously adding support for more wallets. Check back regularly for updates.

Buying NFTs

How do I purchase an NFT on Tokenated?

To purchase an NFT on our marketplace:

  1. Connect your wallet to Tokenated.
  2. Browse the marketplace and find an NFT you want to purchase.
  3. Click on the NFT to view its details.
  4. If it's a fixed-price sale, click "Buy Now" and confirm the transaction in your wallet.
  5. If it's an auction, place a bid by entering your offer amount and clicking "Place Bid".
  6. Wait for the transaction to be confirmed on the blockchain.
  7. Once confirmed, the NFT will appear in your collection.

What are gas fees and why do I need to pay them?

Gas fees are payments made to compensate for the computing energy required to process and validate transactions on the blockchain. These fees are necessary for several reasons:

  • They incentivize miners/validators to include your transaction in the next block.
  • They help prevent spam and denial-of-service attacks on the network.
  • They allocate the network's limited resources efficiently.

Gas fees fluctuate based on network congestion. For lower fees, try conducting transactions during off-peak hours.

Creating NFTs

How do I create and mint my first NFT?

Creating your first NFT on Tokenated is simple:

  1. Connect your wallet and navigate to the "Create" page.
  2. Upload your digital asset (image, video, audio, or 3D model).
  3. Fill in the details: name, description, properties, and royalties.
  4. Choose whether to create a single NFT or a collection.
  5. Click "Create" and confirm the transaction in your wallet.
  6. Pay the gas fee to mint your NFT on the blockchain.
  7. Once minted, your NFT will appear in your profile and can be listed for sale.

What file formats are supported for NFTs?

Tokenated supports the following file formats:

  • Images: JPG, PNG, GIF, SVG (max 100MB)
  • Videos: MP4, WEBM (max 1GB)
  • Audio: MP3, WAV, OGG (max 100MB)

For the best experience, we recommend optimizing your files before uploading. Large files may take longer to display and interact with on the marketplace.

Selling NFTs

How do I list my NFT for sale?

To list your NFT for sale on Tokenated:

  1. Navigate to your profile and select the NFT you want to sell.
  2. Click the "Sell" button on the NFT detail page.
  3. Choose your sale type: Fixed Price or Timed Auction.
  4. For Fixed Price: Set your desired price in ETH or other supported cryptocurrencies.
  5. For Timed Auction: Set a starting price, reserve price (optional), and auction duration.
  6. Set your royalty percentage for secondary sales (between 0-10%).
  7. Click "Complete Listing" and approve the transaction in your wallet.

Your NFT will be listed immediately after blockchain confirmation.

What fees are charged when I sell an NFT?

When selling NFTs on Tokenated, you'll encounter the following fees:

  • Platform Fee: 2.5% of the sale price (deducted automatically).
  • Gas Fees: Variable cost paid to the network for transaction processing.
  • Creator Royalties: If you're reselling an NFT, a percentage (typically 5-10%) goes to the original creator.

All fees are transparent and displayed before you complete your listing. We offer reduced platform fees for high-volume sellers and featured collections.

Security

How does Tokenated ensure my NFTs are secure?

Tokenated prioritizes security through multiple measures:

  • Smart Contract Audits: All platform contracts undergo rigorous third-party security audits.
  • Decentralized Storage: NFT metadata and assets are stored on IPFS for permanent decentralized access.
  • Verified Collections: Blue checkmarks indicate verified creators and collections to prevent fraud.
  • Non-Custodial: We never take custody of your NFTs or private keys.
  • Regular Security Updates: Our platform undergoes continuous security improvements.

How can I avoid NFT scams?

To protect yourself from NFT scams, follow these safety tips:

  • Research creators before purchasing by reviewing their history and social media presence.
  • Be wary of deals that seem too good to be true or create urgency.
  • Never share your wallet's seed phrase or private keys with anyone.
  • Disconnect your wallet from websites when not in use.
  • Double-check all transaction details before confirming.
  • Use a hardware wallet for storing valuable NFTs.

Remember: Tokenated staff will never ask for your private keys or seed phrase.

When do I receive payment for my sold NFTs?

When your NFT sells, the payment process is as follows:

  • Fixed Price Sales: You receive payment immediately after the transaction is confirmed on the blockchain (typically within minutes).
  • Auction Sales: Payment is released when the auction ends and the highest bidder's payment clears.
