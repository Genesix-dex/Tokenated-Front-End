Imprints are Tokenated's social engagement feature that allows you to show appreciation for NFTs you admire. Similar to "likes" on social platforms, Imprints help creators gain visibility and allow collectors to bookmark their favorite pieces.

To Imprint an NFT, simply click the Imprint icon displayed on any NFT card or detail page.

Found an NFT you love but it's not for sale? Our Request feature allows you to express interest in purchasing NFTs that aren't currently listed, signaling to owners that there's demand for their work.

How it works:

  1. 1. Navigate to an unlisted NFT's detail page
  2. 2. Click the "Request to List" button
  3. 3. Enter an offer price (optional) and a message
  4. 4. The owner receives your request notification
  5. 5. If interested, they can list directly from your request

For NFT owners: You'll receive notifications when collectors express interest in your unlisted pieces, allowing you to gauge market demand before deciding to list.

Note: To prevent spam, there's a limit of 10 active requests per user per day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Tokenated and NFTs

NFT stands for Non-Fungible Token. These are unique digital assets that represent ownership of digital items such as art, music, videos, and more on the blockchain.
Browse collections, ensure you have enough cryptocurrency in your wallet, and click the "Buy Now" or place a bid. Once confirmed, the NFT will be transferred to your wallet.
Most NFTs are purchased with ETH (Ethereum), but some marketplaces also accept other cryptocurrencies like WETH, SOL, or platform-specific tokens.
Place a bid on an NFT auction. If you're the highest bidder when the auction ends, the NFT will be transferred to your wallet after payment.
Check the NFT's smart contract address, verify the creator's wallet, and review the transaction history on the blockchain to ensure authenticity.
Connect your wallet, select the NFT you want to sell, set a price or auction parameters, and confirm the listing. You'll need to approve the marketplace contract first.
Royalties are fees paid to the original creator whenever their NFT is resold on the secondary market, typically ranging from 5-10% of the sale price.
You can choose to list your NFT for a fixed time period or keep it listed until you decide to delist it. Auction durations typically range from 1-7 days.
Research similar NFTs in your collection or category, consider the current market conditions, and set a competitive price. You can also start with an auction to let the market decide.
Create your digital artwork, choose a marketplace, upload your file, fill in metadata (title, description, properties), and mint it on the blockchain.
Most marketplaces support common file types including JPG, PNG, GIF, SVG, MP4, WEBM, MP3, WAV, OGG, GLB, and GLTF. Maximum file sizes vary by platform.
Smart contracts are programs stored on the blockchain that run when predetermined conditions are met. They control the minting, trading, and royalties of your NFTs.
